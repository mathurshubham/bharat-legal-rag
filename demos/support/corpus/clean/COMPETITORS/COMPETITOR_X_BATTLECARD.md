# CompetitorX — Sales Battlecard

**CONFIDENTIAL — SALES/AE USE ONLY. DO NOT SHARE WITH CUSTOMERS.** This battlecard captures the talking points we use when displacing CompetitorX deals. Quoting any of this verbatim to a customer is a fireable offense.

## Their positioning

CompetitorX positions itself as the "enterprise-grade" PM tool — long deployments, complex permission models, deep field customization. They sell into Fortune 500s through long procurement cycles.

## Their weaknesses

- **Slow UI.** Page loads of 3–5 seconds are documented in their G2 reviews — search for "slow" or "laggy" in their last 50 reviews.
- **No public API.** They offer a webhook-only integration layer; no read APIs available outside their professional services bundle.
- **License cost.** Mid-market deals run roughly **3× our list price** at the same seat count, based on the two displacements we did in Q1 2026.
- **Mobile is an afterthought.** Their iOS app has not been updated since Q2 2025; we have working offline mode and they don't.
- **Long deployment.** Their median time-to-value is 9 months; ours is 6 weeks. Show the GA-Bank case study to senior buyers.

## Our advantages

1. **Open API + SCIM provisioning** — bring up CompetitorX is webhook-only.
2. **SOC 2 Type II + ISO 27001** — same as CompetitorX, no compliance gap.
3. **SSO included in Business tier** — they put SSO behind their Enterprise tier only.
4. **Modern UI** — show a side-by-side load-time demo in the discovery call.
5. **6-week onboarding** — versus their 9-month median.

## Objection handling

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "But CompetitorX is the standard for enterprise." | "Standards lag tools. Show me a single dev team that prefers the slower interface." |
| "We've already trained 800 people on CompetitorX." | "We mirror 90% of their keyboard shortcuts; switch cost is two weeks of side-by-side use." |
| "Their custom fields are deeper." | "True. Ask the buyer: how many of those custom fields are actually used in reports?" |
| "Procurement already has their MSA on file." | "Our enterprise MSA has been pre-approved by the legal teams at [reference accounts]; we can move in 7 days." |

## Don'ts

- Don't disparage CompetitorX by name in writing (email, Slack, RFP responses). Speak generally — "legacy enterprise PM tools".
- Don't quote their pricing numbers in writing.
- Don't share this battlecard with prospects, customers, or anyone outside sales/AE.

This battlecard is reviewed monthly by the sales lead. If a talking point lands well, log the call to the `#wins` channel so we can iterate.
