# CompetitorY Battlecard — Sales & AE Use Only

> **⚠️ CONFIDENTIAL — Internal use only. Do not share with prospects, customers, or external parties.**

---

## Their Pitch

CompetitorY sells simplicity. Their go-to-market is all about "no setup, no bloat, just tasks" — and they land well with small teams who are allergic to complexity. Expect to hear:

- *"We're lighter and faster to adopt than the big players."*
- *"You'll be up and running in 10 minutes."*
- *"We're built for modern teams, not enterprises."*

That last line is the tell. They're pitching away from enterprise before you even ask. When a prospect is evaluating both of us, CompetitorY is betting the deal stays small and informal. Your job is to show what happens when it doesn't.

---

## Their Weaknesses

These are documented, verifiable gaps — not FUD. Lean on them when the conversation turns to security, compliance, or scale.

### No SSO
CompetitorY has no SAML, no OIDC, no Google Workspace SSO. Every user logs in with a standalone username and password. For any company with an IdP (Okta, Azure AD, Google Workspace), this means:

- No centralized access control
- No automatic deprovisioning when employees leave
- Instant compliance red flag for IT and security teams

### No SOC 2
CompetitorY has not completed a SOC 2 Type II audit. They have no ISO 27001 certification either. For prospects in fintech, healthcare, legal, or any regulated vertical, this is a hard blocker — not a preference. Their security posture is self-attested, not independently verified.

### Weak Role-Based Permissions
CompetitorY's permissions model is essentially binary: admin or member. There's no granular project-level access control, no guest roles, no ability to restrict sensitive projects to specific team members. At 10+ users this becomes a liability. At 50+ it's unmanageable.

## Our Advantages

Lead with security and compliance. That's where the deal is won against CompetitorY.

### Security & Compliance Stack

Acme Tasks ships with granular admin controls, project-level permissions, and guest access. Business tier supports up to 50 seats with full admin tooling. Enterprise is unlimited seats with SCIM-driven provisioning. CompetitorY's binary permission model doesn't survive contact with a real org chart.

Business tier: 99.9% uptime SLA. Enterprise: 99.95%. CompetitorY publishes no SLA. If a prospect's procurement team asks for an SLA commitment, the conversation is over before it starts on their side.

## Objection Handling

**"CompetitorY is cheaper."**
Ask what their IT or security team thinks of a tool with no SSO and no SOC 2. The total cost of a breach, a failed audit, or a manual offboarding process dwarfs the per-seat delta. Our Pro tier starts at $19/seat — the gap is smaller than they think, and the Business tier at $49/seat includes SSO, audit log, and a 99.9% SLA.

**"We don't need SSO yet."**
"Yet" is doing a lot of work in that sentence. Every company that's been burned by a departed employee's active account said the same thing. SSO isn't a feature you adopt when you need it — it's infrastructure you put in place before you need it. We make it trivial to set up now.

**"Our security team hasn't flagged it."**
Offer to loop in their security team directly. Hand them our SOC 2 Type II report and ISO 27001 certificate. Ask CompetitorY for the same. The conversation tends to end there.

**"We're a small team, compliance doesn't apply to us."**
If they have a single enterprise customer, a single healthcare client, or a single government contract in their pipeline, it applies to them. We grow with them; CompetitorY doesn't.

**"CompetitorY is simpler to use."**
Acme Tasks v4.2 ships with keyboard shortcuts, templates, and a kanban board on every paid tier. Simplicity isn't their moat — it's a positioning choice. Book a 20-minute demo and let the product speak.
