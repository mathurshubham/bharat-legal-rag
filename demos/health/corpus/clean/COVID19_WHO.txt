COVID-19 — WHO FACT SHEET

Key Facts

COVID-19 is a disease caused by the SARS-CoV-2 coronavirus with common symptoms including fever, chills, and sore throat. Most individuals recover fully without intervention. The WHO documents nearly 780 million reported cases and over 7.1 million deaths globally since December 2019. Over 13.64 billion vaccine doses were administered by end of 2024. COVID-19 remains a significant public health challenge with continued virus circulation.

Overview

The SARS-CoV-2 virus spreads through respiratory particles in close contact or shared indoor spaces. Transmission also occurs via contaminated surfaces touching eyes, nose, or mouth.

Due to immunity from prior infections and vaccination, most people recover without treatment. However, about 6% develop post COVID-19 condition (PCC, or long COVID) causing persistent fatigue, memory problems, and breathing difficulties affecting daily functioning.

Higher-risk populations include older adults, those with underlying conditions, and unvaccinated individuals. Specific risk factors include diabetes, heart or lung disease, obesity, and compromised immune systems.

Symptoms

Most Common: Fever, chills, sore throat.

Less Common: Muscle aches, fatigue, runny or blocked nose, headache, sore eyes, dizziness, persistent cough, chest tightness or pain, shortness of breath, hoarse voice, appetite loss, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, diarrhea, taste or smell changes, sleep difficulty.

Severe Symptoms Requiring Immediate Medical Attention: Difficulty breathing or inability to speak in sentences; confusion; drowsiness or unconsciousness; persistent chest pain or pressure; cold, clammy, pale, or bluish skin; speech or movement loss.

Severe COVID-19 consequences include respiratory failure, sepsis, blood clots, and organ failure.

Transmission

SARS-CoV-2, the virus causing COVID-19, spreads through the air via infectious respiratory particles when infected persons breathe, talk, cough, or sneeze. Transmission increases in close proximity or enclosed spaces.

Symptoms typically emerge 3-6 days after exposure and last up to 10 days. Infected individuals spread the virus even without symptoms or before symptom onset.

Treatment

Medical treatments exist for COVID-19, though most mild cases resolve without intervention. Healthcare providers tailor treatment based on disease severity, progression risk, patient age, symptoms, coexisting conditions, and factors affecting treatment compatibility.

Prevention

For Symptomatic or Positive Individuals: Remain home, away from others; avoid close contact with high-risk persons; wear properly fitted masks around others; cover mouth and nose when coughing or sneezing; maintain frequent hand hygiene; improve indoor ventilation.

To Avoid Infection: Maintain distance from those with respiratory symptoms; avoid crowded or poorly ventilated spaces if high-risk; wear masks if unavoidable; maintain frequent hand hygiene.

For Severe Illness Prevention: Vaccination is recommended, especially for high-risk individuals and healthcare workers.
