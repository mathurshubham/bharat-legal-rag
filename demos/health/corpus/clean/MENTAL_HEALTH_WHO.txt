MENTAL DISORDERS — WHO FACT SHEET

Key Facts

Nearly 1 in 7 people globally live with a mental disorder. Mental disorders can severely disrupt daily life and cause long-term suffering without treatment. Effective prevention and treatment options exist, yet most people lack access to care.

Overview

A mental disorder involves a clinically significant disturbance in an individual's cognition, emotional regulation, or behaviour. In 2021, approximately 1.1 billion people worldwide experienced mental disorders, with anxiety and depressive conditions being most prevalent.

Types of Mental Disorders

Anxiety Disorders: Affecting 359 million people in 2021, these involve excessive fear and worry with significant functional impairment. Several subtypes exist, including generalized anxiety disorder and panic disorder.

Depression: Impacting 280 million people in 2019, depression involves depressed mood or lost interest in activities lasting at least two weeks, accompanied by concentration problems, guilt, and sleep disruption.

Bipolar Disorder: Experienced by 37 million people, this condition features alternating depressive and manic episodes with increased activity and impulsivity.

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder: Develops after exposure to threatening events, characterized by intrusive memories, avoidance behaviors, and heightened threat perception lasting weeks.

Schizophrenia: Affecting approximately 23 million people, this disorder involves significant impairments in perception and changes in behaviour, including delusions and hallucinations.

Eating Disorders: 16 million people experienced eating disorders in 2021, involving abnormal eating patterns and body image concerns.

Disruptive Behavior Disorders: Affecting 41 million people, these involve persistent defiant or rule-violating behaviors.

Neurodevelopmental Disorders: Including intellectual development disorders, autism spectrum disorder, and ADHD, these arise during development and affect cognitive or behavioral functioning.

Treatment

Treatment varies by disorder type and severity. Options include psychological interventions, medication, cognitive behavioral therapy, family-based approaches, and psychosocial rehabilitation.

Prevention and Risk Factors

Individual, family, community, and structural factors influence mental health protection. People exposed to poverty, violence, disability, and inequality face elevated risk.
